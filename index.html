ClearSEP
Unleashing the Power of Authentically Mixed Audio Tracks

Abstract

Universal sound separation aims to disentangle mixed audio into clean tracks corresponding to distinct events, which holds significant importance for artificial intelligence auditory perception. However, existing works face several challenges in training sound separation models: (1) It is difficult to annotate large-scale audio data with fine-grained labels, limiting the effective alignment between queries and audio tracks. (2) Audio often contains multiple mixed tracks, making it challenging to align events with their corresponding tracks. This paper introduces a practical sound separation model, ClearSound, designed to unlock the potential of large-scale mixed-track data for robust sound separation. Specifically, we investigate the strengths and limitations of using labels versus captions as queries for sound separation. We propose a label-centric self-labeling process for unlabeled/mixed-track data, which expands the clean-track audio data available for sound separation to 78M tracks. Furthermore, we present a Silence-Aug strategy that enhances the purity of separated tracks. Experiments across multiple datasets demonstrate that our model achieves state-of-the-art sound separation performance. The detailed experimental results are presented in https://clearsound.github.io.
